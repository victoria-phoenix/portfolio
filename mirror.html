the project timeline

A 2 week design scope posed limitations, and we foresaw 2 potential challenges within the research phase of our product lifecycle: 

  1. open conversation
  2. participant seeking

With the tight timeframe, there was only 1 try to obtain the insights needed to design out our app. To circumvent ill-preparedness, we continually referenced levels of hierarchical empathy to help draw out unadulterated insights throughout our research.



in-store vs. online shopping

Key Insight #1: There still remains a fairly equal split between shopping in physical stores vs. online retailers. 55% of online shoppers would prefer to buy from a merchant with a physical store presence over an online-only retailer. 51% of Americans think shopping online is the best way to shop, with 49% preferring shopping in-store. Key Insight #2 With information right at our fingertips, shoppers are more fickle with their brands than ever before. 72% of young shoppers research online before purchasing in a store. With millennials, "89% of mobile users say they search the web while shopping." market research confirms unified channel strategies (online + physical) to be most practical and suggests a "best-of-both worlds" approach. Our challenge: buildout of a user-centric responsive website whilst ensuring successful e-commerce market penetration

Proving & Disapproving Assumptions Customers know what they want & roughly how much it should cost 1 user knew exactly what he wanted when purchasing clothes online, 3 users typically browse for sale items / do searches and filters, oftentimes skeptical of purchases because they can't see it in person. Shoppers will be more stringent when shopping online, expecting prices to be cheaper versus in store True, from price comparisons to looking for free shipping options to only shopping at sales areas, all 4 users were more stringent shopping online. Online shopping is more a personalized experience versus in-store Inconclusive, however, users def. want more suggestive features, with 3 of 4 stating it would make their experience more pleasant. Uncovering Unknowns What motivates a customer to purchase clothes online as opposed to in store? Multiple factors, however, all 4 users have talked about the following: Comparative shopping allows for cheaper price Lack of time to shop in store Not having to deal with poor customer service Lack of conveniency shopping in stores - overwhelming, crowded and poorly categorized Time consuming process of waiting for checklist / fitting rooms What are their typical shopping processes from product searching to product purchasing? Email Updates -> Browse -> Cart & Contemplate -> Purchase -> Try On -> Keep Good, Return Bad Straight to sales catalog -> filter and sort to specifications -> Cart and purchase Navigate menu categories and uses sorts (low to high prices) -> cart and contemplate -> purchase Offline and then online, but only if absolutely necessary. (Eg. can't find right size in store).

accumulated insights

Users need to be reassured that they are making good purchases Users need to save time and money when purchasing online Users want sites to offer item suggestions that match their clothing style Users are skeptical of products purchased online because they can't see it in person Users do not want to pay extra for shipping & returning items and expect to save money online Users want their shopping experience to be personalized
