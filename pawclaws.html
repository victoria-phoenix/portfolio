the technology

Aria is a combination wearable + mobile app solution to asthma management. Data aggregates from the wearable patch into a mobile application so to display vital information to asthmatic users in a succinct and user friendly manner.

wearable tracking capabilities:

  • heart-rate
  • wheezing + coughing
  • respiration
  • exercise

research goal
elicit enough knowledge to freely communicate and truly listen to what asthmatics have to say so to maximize on deep insights from user interactions



We kickoff the project with research plan. Series of questions guided the initial research phase: We kickoff the project with research plan. Series of questions guided the initial research phase: Which platforms are currently available for potential adopters? What can we learn from them? What information is potential searched for? What information is helpful? Beginning stages of the adoption process and the associated thoughts of the adopter? What factors do they use to decide on the specific pet that they want (or do not want)? How and why did the adopter decide that they wanted to become a pet owner? The online pet finding experience: What information is lacking? Once a potential pet is located, how is interest communicated, and how is a meeting scheduled?



the competitors which pet adoption sites / platforms are available for shelters and what can we learn from them? An analysis of the 3 direct and 2 indirect competitors reveals ample search filters, elaborate fill-ins for pet information, and of course detailed location and point of contact for shelters.

From a round of ideation and white-boarding, we fleshed out ideas and cross-compared according to business and user needs, as well as costs. many ideas, too little time. We underwent a quick brainstorming session wherein ideas were cross-compared business and user goals as well as technological feasibilities. These ideas were then dissipated down to a list of features to be implemented within the site. Our focus we on providing ample information for users to find their new family member, and as suggested by user insights, on humanizing the adoptee.

goal #2: implement cutoffs

in an effort to prevent feature creep, features were in relation to the results of a conducted competitive analysis



With features set in place, we played around with the architecture and site flows. mapped the architecture and fleshed out the layout via iterative rounds of low level sketches before moving forward with the time-consuming high level prototype. With the mid-fidelity, a prototype was created to test the overall usability of the site.
